Butternut Squash Soup

Method:

  1. Heat The oven to 200C.
  2. Cut the squash into large cubes, about 4cm, then toss in a large roasting tin with 1 tbsp of the olive oil.
  3. Roast for 30 mins, turning once during cooking, until golden and soft.
  4. While the butternut squash cooks, melt the butter with the remaining 1 tbsp olive oil in a large saucepan, then add the onions, garlic clove and threee-quarters of the chillies.
  5. Cover and cook on a very low heat for 15-20 mins until the onions are completely soft.
  6. Tip the butternut squash into the pan, add the stock and creme fraitche, then whizz with a stick blender until smooth. For a really silky soup, put the soap into a liquidiser and blitz it in batches.
  7. Return to the pan, gently reheat, then season to taste. Serve the soap in bowls with swirls of creme fraitche and a scattering of the remaining chopped chilli.
